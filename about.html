OK. What's to know?

I'm a diverse mixture of many loves and curiosities. These include; Philosophy, Politics, Robotics, Ethics, Symbolic Logic, Rock climbing, Weight Lifting, Animals...you get the picture. It's rare to find something I'm not interested in.

I studied Philosophy and Cognitive Science at university, funding my way through it working in the fitness industry; training, motivating and (hopefully) inspiring my clients.

I loved my degree because it shaped me as a person, gave me an unquenchable thirst for knowledge and taught me how to see through the quagmire of bullshit we find ourselves facing on a daily basis. I love my job because I get to help people achieve awesome things. I also love it because when I go to work, I get to make a positive contribution to my clients' lives.

And there it is, my ethos for life.

In with:

  • Knowledge
  • Awesome things
  • Positive Contributions

Out with:

  • Bullshit

So what's the plan?

If there's one thing in life that terrifies me more than anything else, it's boredom. Consequently I tend to have a myriad of projects running concurrently. Right now Indie Phone is my top priority but it's by no means the only thing that matters to me.

I initially became involved within Indie Phone as a mentee of Aral Balkan and Laura Kalbag, both of whom taught me a great deal about web development and design. These are skills I hope to continue building on, along with learning as much about the development of new technologies and innovations, as I navigate my way through the tech world.

Keen to be as multifaceted an individual as possible, I am currently preparing to enter my first bodybuilding competition in early 2015. My good intentions are to blog my way through the entire process; from the physical, to the psychological and even the philosophical aspects of the journey.

Of course, throughout, I shall continue to cuddle dogs so enthusiastically that they raise one eyebrow and back away slowly; approach street cats so eagerly that they roll their eyes and scan for the nearest tree; and generally make a nuisance of myself to anything with four legs and fur...and some things without.
