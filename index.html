Who am I?

Hi there, thanks for stopping by. My name's Jo. I'm 26, I live in Brighton and this, right here, is my first website. Built from scratch. Properly. It may not be much right now, but over the coming months it's going to grow into something amazing.

What am I doing?

I'm currently taking my first, tentative steps on the journey towards web developing. I, like many others (I suspect) had always been fascinated by web design, but assumed I wasn't smart enough and was afraid I'd fail. I studied Cognitive Science at university, but avoided courses like 'Introduction to Programming' for fear of not understanding and looking like a fool. I wish I'd figured it out long ago, but eventually I realised, fear is not an excuse and hey, guess what? It turns out, I am smart enough.

I'm 26 years old, I have a degree in Philosophy and Cognitive Science and right now I'm taking my first, tentative steps on the journey towards web developing.

"Why?" I hear you ask. "Surely with a degree in Philosophy you have a ready made career waiting for you, society is literally crying out for young, female philosophers to make sense of life, the universe and everything. There must be a red leather Chesterfield armchair somewhere just waiting for your arse to be imprinted on it."

You'd think. I have learned a lot of things from my degree, some of which I will come to talk about as I develop this page. I'm particularly interested in political philosophy, ethics and logic. The first two I hope to write about as time allows, the last, I hope, will provide me with the much needed grounding to go from WebDev Novice to WebDev Ninja at record speed. However, perhaps the most poignant nugget of wisdom I obtained during my degree was that society, as a whole, is oblivious as to the enormous value of philosophy (Again, I'll return to this later).
